# Usage & Billing

Every request your proxy function handles counts as one **invocation**. Your plan includes a monthly invocation allowance, and this page explains how that works, what happens when you go over, and how to track your usage.

## Included Invocations Per Plan

| Plan | Monthly Invocations Included |
|------|------------------------------|
| Pro | 5,000 |
| Enterprise | Custom |

These invocations are shared across all proxy functions in your organization.

## How Overage Billing Works

EnvManager uses a **soft limit** model. Reaching your plan's included invocations does not immediately stop your proxies from working. Instead:

1. Requests continue to be accepted past your limit
2. Overage usage is tracked and billed at the end of the billing period
3. A **10x safety valve** is in place -- if your usage reaches 10 times your plan's included amount, requests are blocked until the next billing period or until you upgrade

For example, on the Pro plan (5,000 included invocations), the safety valve triggers at 50,000 invocations.

::callout{icon="i-lucide-info" color="info"}
The 10x safety valve protects you from unexpected charges due to runaway scripts or abuse. If you anticipate legitimate high usage, upgrade your plan before hitting the cap.
::

## Viewing Your Usage

You can monitor your current invocation usage from the billing settings page.

::steps
### Go to Settings

Click your organization name or avatar, then select **Settings**.

### Open the Billing Tab

Click the **Billing** tab in the settings sidebar.

### Review Proxy Usage

The billing page shows:

- Invocations used this billing period
- Invocations included in your current plan
- A usage bar showing how close you are to your limit
- Overage invocations if applicable
::

## What Counts as an Invocation

Each incoming HTTP request to a proxy endpoint counts as one invocation, regardless of:

- Whether the request succeeds or fails
- The size of the request or response
- Which proxy function handles it

Requests rejected by rate limiting (`429 Too Many Requests`) still count as invocations.

## Upgrading for More Invocations

If you are regularly hitting your plan limit, upgrading gives you more included invocations and raises the safety valve ceiling.

::steps
### Go to Billing Settings

Navigate to **Settings** > **Billing**.

### Click Upgrade

Click the **Upgrade Plan** button and follow the checkout flow.

### Your new limits apply immediately

Once payment is confirmed, your plan limits update right away. Any overage from the current period is reflected in your next invoice.
::

## Tips for Reducing Invocations

If you are on the Pro plan and want to stay within your limit:

- Enable **rate limiting** on high-traffic proxies to cap per-minute usage (see [Rate Limiting](/docs/proxy-functions/rate-limiting))
- Review the [Analytics Dashboard](/docs/proxy-functions/analytics) to identify which proxies use the most invocations
- Avoid calling proxies from automated scripts or bots unless that traffic is intentional
