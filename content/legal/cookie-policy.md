## 2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit our website or use our application. These files contain information about your browsing activity, preferences, and session information. Each cookie typically includes:

- A unique identifier

- Information about your browser and device

- The domain from which the cookie originated

- The expiration date of the cookie

We also use similar tracking technologies, including:

- Web beacons and pixels: Small transparent images embedded in web pages that track page visits and user interactions

- Local storage: Browser storage mechanisms that function similarly to cookies but with larger storage capacity

Analytics cookies require your explicit consent and can be disabled through the cookie consent mechanism.

## 7. How to Control and Delete Cookies

In addition to our cookie consent interface, you can control cookies through your browser settings. Most modern browsers allow you to:

- View cookies stored on your device

- Delete all or specific cookies

- Block cookies from specific websites

- Enable 'Do Not Track' (DNT) signals

- Use private/incognito browsing modes that typically do not store persistent cookies

Browser-Specific Instructions:

Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data

Mozilla Firefox: Preferences > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data

Microsoft Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Clear browsing data

Apple Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data

Note: Disabling essential cookies may prevent certain features of EnvManager from functioning properly. Non-essential cookies can typically be disabled without affecting core functionality.
